Differences: 
Overloaded operators do not maintain evaluation ordering or short circuiting like built in operators.
Overloaded operators do not perform conversions in some situations that normal operators would.
Overloaded operators may not perform similar functions to their built-in counterparts

Similarities:
They can operate on built in and class types.
They can take the same number of arguments as their built in operator counterparts.
You may only overload operators which are built-in
